    6. Subject 114 (the high Akkermansia subject) has zero Faecalibacterium
    7. Subject 114 also has low plasma choline
    8. on the other hand, subject 110 Pre and Post Med had high choline and high Faecalibacterium
    9. Subject 116 (high baseline meat diet), has high Cortisol-21-sulfate, highest Pre and Post FF diet, lower Pre and Post Med
    10. Subject 116 also had high Ruminococcaceae_NK4A214_group, which was higher Pre and Post FF than Pre and Post Med
    11. Glycodeoxycholic acid negatively associated with Erysipelotrichaceae_UCG-003
    12. Creatinine positively associated with Lachnospiraceae_ND3007_group, negatively with Ruminococcus_1
    13. L-proprionylcarnitine negatively associated with Blautia, positively with Bifidobacteria
    14. indole-6-carboxaldehyde is high in Subject 114 Pre Med, also high in this subject at this time point: Varibaculum, Anaerococcus, Negativicoccus, Finegoldia
    15. Look at graph Indole-6-carboxaldehyde vs. Dysgonomonas: see almost perfect positive association between this bacterium and this metabolite in Subject 114 only! Looks like Pre Med to Post Med change is: high Dysgonomonas and high indole-6-carboxaldehyde, then Dysgonomonas goes almost to zero and indole-6-carboxaldehyde goes down by 100%
    16. This looks like it could be a direct relationship: that Dysgonomonas produces indole-6-carboxaldehyde, and that presence of Varibaculum, Anaerococcus, Negativicoccus, Finegoldia help induce that process somehow
    17. Ruminococcus_1 and Ruminococcus_2 negatively associated with Kynurenine, Coprococcus_3 and Parabecteroides positively associated

    22. Oleoyl-L-carnitine positively associated with Lachnospiraceae_ND3007_group and Roseburia, negatively associated with Bifidobacterium and Fusicatenibacter
    23. 1-O-Hexadecyl-2-O-(2E-butenoyl)-sn-glyceryl-3-phosphocholine negatively associated with Bilophila and positively with Lachnospiraceae_ND3007_group

    7. In subject 112 Pre FF to Post FF TMAO decreases from 64,000 to 22,000, Alloprevotella also decreases; Pre Med to Post Med TMAO decreases from 30,000 to 21,000 and again Alloprevotella decreases; everyone else has zero Alloprevotella at all time points

Baseline Food Intakes

  • Microbiome Correlation with Baseline Food Intakes
    Show the Notes
    1. Baseline dairy intake is positively associated with Bilophila on the FF diet but not on the Med diet.
    2. Same is true for Dorea
    3. But opposite is true for Veilonella
    4. This suggests that higher baseline dairy intake predisposes for a Bilophila “bloom” in the context of a high saturated fat diet
    5. Baseline vegetable intake negatively associated with Ruminococcus_NK4A214_group on Med diet but not on FF diet.
    6. This bacterium seems to be increased in mice with type 2 diabetes and is decreased by inulin intake: peerj.com/articles/4446/
    7. Subject 109 had very high intake of desserts, about double that of everyone else in the study
    8. Subject 110 had very high egg, fruit and legume intake, about 9X that of everyone else; also very high alcohol intake!
    9. Vegetable intake is a nice variable because we have a nice spread across the range from 0 g/d to 800 g/d
    10. Subject 113 also had high legume intake
    11. Subject 116 had very high meat intake
    12. Subjects 108, 112, 114, 116 and 118 had either zero or very low intake of coffee/tea whereas 107, 109, 110, 111, and 113 had between 200-500 g/d coffee/tea intake; it would be interesting to see whether when you make the subjects into groups (coffee/tea drinkers vs. non-drinkers) whether you get significant differences in microbiota and biogenic amines
