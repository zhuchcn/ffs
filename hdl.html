  • Comparison of Normalization Methods for Lipidomcis Data

    • Among the three normalization method, simply taking the proportion of each lipid feature yields the highest number of features that have significant differential abbundence (P<=0.05).

    • However, using concentration directly yields the most features that are significantly correlated with HDL Cholesterol Efflux (Pearsonâ€™s correlation test, P<=0.05).

    • Normalizing by total protein yields the most features that are significanly correlated with TNFa (same method as above)

    • During the lipidomics analysis when HDL samples were injected into the MS, the HDL total proteins are not the same accross samples. HDL lipid/protein ratio also varies between samples, so normalizing by total protein maybe not propriate.

    • Using HDL cholesterol concentration measured by kit is an alternative solution, but not sure it will better.

    • Among the 3 normalization methods so far, I vote for using Proportion.

  • HDL Lipidome Lipid Satuation

    • HDL lipidome lipid satuation is significated effected by 3-days diet intervantion. We calculated the Equivalent Of Double bond per 18 carbons (EOD-18) as below: \[ EOD18 = \left( \frac{\sum_{i=1}^n Abd_i \times ND_i }{\sum_{i=1}^n Abd_i \times NC_i} \right) \times 18 \] \(Abd_i\) is the abundance of the \(i^{th}\) lipid species. \(ND_i\) is the number of double bond of this lipid speceis, while \(NC_i\) is the number of carbon.

    • The EOD-18 of the whole HDL lipidome is 1.51 accross all lipid species and lipid classes, which means there are around one to two double bond per 18 carbons. Cholesterol Ester has the highest EOD-18 (2.37), followed by PE (1.98), and PC (1.45), while TG, SM, Ceramide, and LPC have very small EOD-18 (0.99, 0.74, 0.65, and 0.47) which suggests satuated fatty acids are dominating in these lipid classes.

    • HDL lipidome global EOD-18 is significanly decreased after fast food and increased after Mediterranean diet (P=0.008). The lipid classes with a high EOD-18, including PE, CE, and PC, are also significantly effected by this diet intervention (P=0.002, 0.005, and 0.006). TG, SM, Ceramide, and LPC have small EOD-18, and they were not significantly effected. PE, PC, CE, and global EOD-18 are also significantly correlated with dietary MUFA and PUFA intake (P<0.05)

  • HDL Lipidome Lipid Chain Length

    • Similar to the EOD18, we calculated the ACL (Average Chain Lenght) as below: \[ACL = \frac {\sum_{i=1}^n Abd_i \times NC_i}{\sum_{i=1}^n Abd_i} \]

    • HDL lipidome global ACL is 18.06, which represents the average fatty acid carbon charin length. SM has the largest ACL (18.68), followed by PE (18.65), CE(18.50), and PC (17.96), while TG, LPC and Ceramide have smaller ACL values (17.35, 17.31, and 17.24).

    • HDL lipidome global ACL is significantly effected by intervention as it decrease after fast food, and increased after Mediterranean diet (P=0.006). CE and PC are also significantly effected as the same pattern as the global ACL (P=0.001, and 0.006). Global ACL is also significantly and positively correlated HDL cholesterol efflux capacity.

    • The ACL of CE is significantly and positively correlated with dietary MUFA and PUFA (P=0.019 and 0.032), while SM is possitively correlated with MUFA (0.041) and PC with PUFA (P=0.039)

  • HDL Lipidome Lipid Class Ratio
    • We also found that the CE/FC ratio is positively correlated with HDL cholesterol efflux (P=0.006, r=0.429). CE/FC ratio is also positively correlated with HDL SAA content (P=0.001, r=0.49).
